Welcome — I study how novels think with the law.

About

I’m a scholar of modernist and contemporary global Anglophone literature. My research examines how the novel engages legal history to reimagine rights, recognition, and justice. Trained as a practicing lawyer in Australia, I bring experience with legal rhetoric and documentary archives to literary study and to teaching in the legal humanities.

Research

My dissertation reads twentieth- and twenty-first-century fiction alongside case law, parliamentary reports, and legal theory to show how narrative forms test the limits of personhood and redress. One chapter traces contemporary engagements with the slave narrative (including work by Zadie Smith and Yaa Gyasi) to ask who holds the right to history and how fiction unsettles legal categories.
